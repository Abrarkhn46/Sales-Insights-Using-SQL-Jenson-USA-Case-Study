# Sales-Insights-Using-SQL-Jenson-USA-Case-Study
Sales Insights Using SQL
 Project Overview

This project analyzes retail sales data of a cycling company (Jenson USA) using SQL. The objective is to extract actionable business insights from customer, product, store, staff, and order datasets to support strategic decision-making.

The analysis focuses on revenue trends, customer behavior, product performance, and operational efficiency.

 Business Objectives

Identify top-selling and high-revenue products

Analyze customer spending behavior

Evaluate store-level sales performance

Measure staff productivity

Detect inventory gaps and unsold products

 Tools & Technologies

SQL (Joins, Subqueries, CTEs)

Window Functions

Aggregate Functions

Ranking Functions (RANK, DENSE_RANK)

EXISTS / NOT EXISTS

 Key Analysis Performed
 Store Performance

Total products sold by each store

Store-wise operational performance

 Product Insights

Top 3 most sold products

Highest revenue product per category

Highest-priced product per category

Products never ordered

Median product price calculation

 Customer Analysis

Customer with highest total spending

Orders per customer per store

Customers who purchased from every category

 Staff Performance

Staff members with above-average sales

Staff with no sales

 Key Insights

Identified top-performing products for inventory and promotion planning.

Detected high-value customers for loyalty targeting.

Evaluated staff performance for operational improvements.

Highlighted unsold products to optimize inventory strategy.

 Outcome

This project demonstrates strong SQL proficiency in:

Complex joins

Window functions

Business-oriented querying

Analytical thinking for retail datasets
